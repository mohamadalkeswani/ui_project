Hama

The area around Hama has been inhabited since 10,000 BC. Inhabited throughout the 2nd century BC, individual traces date back to the Paleolithic period.[2] During excavations from 1931 to 1938, Danish archaeologists discovered traces of settlement from the 5th millennium BC and a total of twelve layers dating back to the Islamic period on the main hill on the northern outskirts of modern Hama. The tell was 336 m long, 215 m wide and 46 m high.[3]
